Unified World Models: Coupling Video and Action Diffusion
for Pretraining on Large Robotic Datasets

Chuning Zhu1, Raymond Yu1, Siyuan Feng2, Benjamin Burchfiel 2, Paarth Shah2, Abhishek Gupta1
1 University of Washington 2 Toyota Research Institute
Paper Code
Unified World Models (UWM) is a single framework that uses separate diffusion timesteps for actions and video to flexibly learn policies, forward/inverse dynamics, and video prediction from both action-labeled and unlabeled data in one unified transformer.

Motivation

Scaling imitation learning is limited by the need for large numbers of high-quality demonstrations, yet there’s a vast amount of unlabeled video data depicting real-world robot interactions that remains untapped. Conventional methods can’t directly harness these videos because they lack action annotations. Unified World Models (UWM) bridge this gap by treating action-free videos as “fully noised” on the action dimension, letting us pretrain a multi-functional diffusion model that robustly captures both policy and temporal dynamics. This approach not only expands our training data but also leads to stronger generalization under domain shifts, since UWM naturally learns forward/inverse dynamics and action distributions in one integrated framework.

Method

Unified World Models (UWM) fuse action diffusion and video diffusion in one transformer, with separate timesteps for each modality. At training time, we sample these timesteps independently at random, exposing the model to all combinations. At inference time, we can flexibly sample from the marginal and conditional distributions by controlling the modality-specific timesteps. Speficially, UWM can represent a policy, a forward dynamics model, an inverse dynamics model, and a video prediction model in one unified framework.

Unified Training On Robot and Video Data

Our training process can flexibly integrates (o, a, o') triplets from robot trajectories alongside (o, o') tuples from unlabeled videos, using the diffusion timestep as a mask. For each mini-batch:

  • Robot Trajectories: The diffusion timesteps for both action and next-frame are randomly sampled. We learn to predict the correct action and next observation noises conditioned on the current observation and diffusion timesteps.
  • Action-Free Videos: We treat the missing actions as fully noised, manually setting the action diffusion timestep to T. This encourages the model to rely on visual cues and learn robust representations, even in the absence of explicit action labels.

Forward Dynamics

One of the core capabilities of UWM is modeling forward dynamics, that is, predicting how the environment changes given the current observation and action. We achieve this by setting the action diffusion timestep to zero, effectively conditioning on the true action, and allowing the video diffusion process to denoise the future frame. This enables UWM to forecast plausible next observations, grounded in both action and visual context. Inverse Dynamics

We evaluate the inverse dynamics mode of UWM on trajectory tracking, where we provide a reference expert trajectory and query the inverse dynamics model to track it. Specifically, we condition the model on consecutive frame pairs and predict the action taken between them. This allows UWM to reproduce expert behavior without relying on a full policy rollout. We test this approach on 50 expert trajectories from the LIBERO dataset. The table below shows success rates for both inverse dynamics tracking and policy rollouts under different time budgets.

We observe that given the same time limit as the trajectory length, the inverse dynamics model achieves a higher success rate than the policy. Policies tend to drift from the reference trajectory but often recover over time. This explains why performance improves under longer time horizons like 1000 steps.

Scalability with Data

To study UWM's ability to scale with pretraining, we train UWM and DP on the task-specific expert demonstrations from scratch. We find that UWM and DP perform similarly when trained from scratch. However, UWM scales from pretraining more effectively than DP.
