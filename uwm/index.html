Unified World Models: Coupling Video and Action Diffusion
for Pretraining on Large Robotic Datasets

Chuning Zhu1, Raymond Yu1, Siyuan Feng2, Benjamin Burchfiel 2, Paarth Shah2, Abhishek Gupta1
1 University of Washington 2 Toyota Research Institute
Paper Code
Unified World Models (UWM) is a single framework that uses separate diffusion timesteps for actions and video to flexibly learn policies, forward/inverse dynamics, and video prediction from both action-labeled and unlabeled data in one unified transformer.

Motivation

Scaling imitation learning is limited by the need for large numbers of high-quality demonstrations, yet there’s a vast amount of unlabeled video data depicting real-world robot interactions that remains untapped. Conventional methods can’t directly harness these videos because they lack action annotations. Unified World Models (UWM) bridge this gap by treating action-free videos as “fully noised” on the action dimension, letting us pretrain a multi-functional diffusion model that robustly captures both policy and temporal dynamics. This approach not only expands our training data but also leads to stronger generalization under domain shifts, since UWM naturally learns forward/inverse dynamics and action distributions in one integrated framework.

How Does UWM Learn from Both Actions and Videos?

Unified World Models (UWM) fuse action diffusion and video diffusion in one transformer, with separate timesteps for each modality. This lets us:

  • Generate actions (policy) by masking future frames.
  • Predict forward dynamics by fixing actions.
  • Infer inverse dynamics by conditioning on frames.
  • Leverage unlabeled video by fully noising the action channel.

Unified Training With & Without Actions

Our training process integrates (observation, action, next-observation) triplets from demonstrations alongside unlabeled video frames lacking explicit actions. During each mini-batch:

  • Action-Labeled Samples: The diffusion timesteps for both action and next-frame are randomly drawn. We learn to reconstruct the correct action and predict the next observation based on the current state.
  • Action-Free Videos: We treat the missing actions as fully noised. This forces the model to rely on visual cues and learn robust representations, even in the absence of explicit action labels.

By sampling from both labeled and unlabeled data during training, the model steadily learns both the action distributions and underlying state transitions. To demonstrate this approach at scale, we train on DROID, a large and diverse set of robotic trajectories that offers multi-environment coverage and varied manipulation tasks. See the paper for more details on dataset composition and preprocessing.

Simulation Experiments

To further evaluate UWM's robustness and scalability, we test it on LIBERO, a challenging robotic manipulation benchmark with diverse tasks. We analyze UWM’s generalization across different object interactions and environment setups.

The LIBERO benchmark contains 100 robotic manipulation tasks, from which we use five distinct evaluation tasks to assess UWM’s ability to generalize.
